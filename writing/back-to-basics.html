Going Back to Basics

28th February 2026

The web is a maelstrom of sort-of-useful frameworks and libraries being pumped out at a dizzying rate that serve to make one's life easier when creating applications or websites. Personally, I have rewritten this site using Svelte, React, and Astro - for no good reason other than as a skill-sharpening exercise such that the web developer section of my brain does not turn into mush. Hence, I feel like I have spent enough time with web frameworks to know when they are useful and when they are overkill.

I have come to the conclusion that a vanilla approach using simple HTML & CSS, with as little JavaScript as possible is by far the best way to build out a personal site for several reasons:

  1. Packaging only vanilla markup, static assets and scripts means you can take advantage of static deployment options, such as Github/Cloudflare pages for free hosting.
  2. Removing all of the abstractions offered by web frameworks allows you to reconnect with the original principles of the web and what a website is actually supposed to be, which turns out to be far simpler than present convention would have you believe.
  3. Simple markup sites load quicker than those that ship dozens of megabites in needless JavaScript.
  4. Relying on only CSS to style the site inspires greater creativity and weakens the reliance on prepackaged libraries and the mess that is Tailwind.

It did occur to me that managing things like blog entries manually would be more challenging without using any sort of database or templating functionality that a framework would provide. However, sometimes the most efficient way is not always best. I would prefer to write these entries as .md's, but there's something pleasantly old-timey about just bashing out plain HTML. Just like the pioneers of old, back when a SPA was somewhere you went for a massage and <marquee> was still cool.

Full disclosure: I love to write Svelte apps and I think the framework is fantastic, but there is something offensive about involving Vite or Webpack to tree-shake my website that consists of 7 html files and a stylesheet. Maybe I am a curmudgeon and a luddite for thinking like this (and my outrage is decades too late), but it saddens me that the web has become less about individuals sharing information on a site that they designed themselves and is now a contest to vibe-code the page with the most impressive scrolling SVG animations that your grandma's 2009 dell optiplex has no hope of rendering before her tea gets cold.
